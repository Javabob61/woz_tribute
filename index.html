Stephen G. Wozniak

"Woz", Cofounder of Apple computers and the PC revolution

Here's a timeline of Woz's life:

  • Born - August 11,1950. San Jose, California.
  • 1968 - Graduated from Homestead High School in Cupertino, California.
  • 1971 - Became friends with Steve Jobs, cofounder of Apple.
  • 1976 - Developed the Apple I computer.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public with its stock value standing at US $117 million.
  • 1981 - Narrowly escapes death while piloting a plane. Needed 2 years to fully recover.
  • 1981-83 - Sponsored two US Festivals to celebrate evolving technologies.
  • 1983 - Resumed duties at Apple, where its stock value reaches $985 million.
  • 1986 - Earned bachelor's degrees in computer science and electrical engineering.
  • 1987 - Retires from regular employment at Apple. Receives an annual stipend of $120,000.

"I had a TV set and a typewriter and that made me think a computer should be laid out like a typewriter with a video screen." -- Steve Wozniak
